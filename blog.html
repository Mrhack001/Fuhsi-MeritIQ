The Federal University of Health Sciences Ila-Orangun (FUHSI) has announced its Post-UTME screening exercise for the 2025/2026 academic session. If you scored 180 and above in the 2025 JAMB examination and selected FUHSI as your institution, this guide contains everything you need to prepare, apply, and succeed in the screening process.

⚡ Quick Summary

FUHSI Post-UTME screening is the second stage of the FUHSI admission process. It is used to assess candidates beyond JAMB scores. Only candidates who meet FUHSI's minimum JAMB cutoff are eligible to register.

What is FUHSI Post-UTME?

Post-UTME (Post Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination) is a supplementary screening exercise conducted by FUHSI to further evaluate prospective students. It assesses academic competence and helps the university rank candidates for admission into various programmes.

The Post-UTME score is combined with your JAMB score and O'Level grades to compute your FUHSI aggregate score, which ultimately determines your admission eligibility.

Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for the FUHSI 2025/2026 Post-UTME screening, you must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Scored 180 or above in the 2025 JAMB/UTME examination
  • Selected FUHSI (Federal University of Health Sciences, Ila-Orangun) as your institution of first or second choice
  • Have a minimum of five (5) credit passes in O'Level (WAEC/NECO/NABTEB), including English Language and Mathematics
  • Have relevant science subjects (Biology/Health Science, Chemistry, Physics) depending on course
  • Must not have exhausted your JAMB registration attempts

How to Register for Post-UTME

1

Visit the FUHSI Admissions Portal

Go to the official FUHSI website at fuhsi.edu.ng and navigate to the Admissions section to access the Post-UTME registration portal.

2

Create / Login to Your Account

Register using your JAMB registration number and other personal details. If you already have an account, log in directly.

3

Pay the Application Fee

Pay the FUHSI Post-UTME screening fee through the approved payment gateway (Remita or as specified). Keep your payment receipt.

4

Complete Your Profile & Upload Documents

Fill in your personal details, course choice, O'Level results, and upload required documents (O'Level result, birth certificate, passport photo).

5

Download & Print Your Examination Slip

After successful registration, download your examination slip showing your date, time, and venue for the screening exercise.

Documents Required

  • JAMB result slip (2025 UTME)
  • O'Level result (WAEC/NECO/NABTEB — original or scratch card)
  • Birth certificate or sworn declaration of age
  • Local government identification or certificate of origin
  • Recent passport photograph (white background)
  • Payment receipt for application fee

What to Expect in the Screening

The FUHSI Post-UTME is typically a computer-based test (CBT) or written examination. It covers the following subject areas depending on your course of choice:

  • English Language — comprehension, grammar, vocabulary
  • Biology / Health Science — core life sciences concepts
  • Chemistry — organic and inorganic chemistry
  • Physics / Mathematics — for applicable programmes

The examination is usually 60–80 questions to be answered within 45–60 minutes. Preparation using past JAMB questions and standard A-level materials is strongly recommended.

Tips to Maximize Your Post-UTME Score

  • Practice with JAMB past questions (Biology, Chemistry, English)
  • Focus on understanding concepts rather than cramming
  • Practice time management — 45 seconds per question on average
  • Arrive at the examination venue early with all required documents
  • Use MeritIQ's eligibility checker to know your target score before the exam

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sit Post-UTME if FUHSI was my second choice?

Yes. Candidates who chose FUHSI as either first or second choice in JAMB are eligible to participate in the Post-UTME screening, provided they meet the minimum score requirement.

What is the minimum JAMB score for FUHSI?

The general minimum is 180, but individual departments may have higher departmental cutoffs. Competitive courses like Medicine and Surgery typically require scores of 280 and above.

Can I apply without O'Level results?

No. You must have a minimum of five credit passes in relevant O'Level subjects to be eligible for Post-UTME registration. However, awaiting result (AR) candidates may be able to apply in some admission cycles — always confirm from the official portal.

One of the most common questions among FUHSI aspirants is: "How exactly is my aggregate score calculated?" Understanding the formula gives you a clear, data-driven picture of where you stand — and what you need to improve before admission decisions are made.

📐 The Short Answer

FUHSI computes aggregate scores using a weighted combination of your JAMB score, Post-UTME performance, and O'Level grades. The exact weights may vary slightly by year and department, but the standard formula is widely documented. For the 2025/2026 and 2026/2027 admission sessions, the Federal University of Health Sciences, Ila-Orangun (FUHSI) calculates aggregate scores using a combined formula of 80% UTME (JAMB) score and 20% O'Level (WAEC/NECO) results. The final score is a total out of 100%, calculated as (JAMB/400 × 80) + (O'Level Points/30 × 20)

The FUHSI Aggregate Formula

Based on available and historical FUHSI admission data, the aggregate score is generally computed as follows:

Component Weight Max Score Contribution
JAMB / UTME Score 80% 400 Up to 80 points
O'Level Grades (Best 5) 20% — Up to 20 points
Total Aggregate 100% — 100 points
📌 Note on Post-UTME

Post-UTME scores at FUHSI are used as a qualifying threshold, not always as a direct aggregate component. However, candidates who perform exceptionally in the Post-UTME may receive additional weighting in borderline admission decisions. Always check the current year's policy from the official FUHSI portal.

How JAMB Score is Converted

Your raw JAMB score (out of 400) is scaled to a 45-point contribution using this formula:

🧮 JAMB Formula

JAMB Points = (Your JAMB Score ÷ 400) × 80

For example, if you scored 260 in JAMB:

  • JAMB Points = (260 ÷ 400) × 80
  • JAMB Points = 0.65 × 80
  • JAMB Points = 52

How O'Level Grades are Scored

O'Level grades are converted to point values based on the standard Nigerian grading system. FUHSI uses the best 5 relevant subjects including English Language and Mathematics:

Grade Points Remark
A1 6 Distinction
B2 5 Very Good
B3 4 Good
C4 3 Credit
C5 2 Credit
C6 1 Credit
D7 0 Fail
E8 0 Fail
F9 0 Fail

The O'Level contribution is calculated as:

🧮 O'Level Formula

O'Level Points = (Average Grade Points of Best 5 Subjects ÷ 30) × 20

Full Worked Example

Let's calculate a complete aggregate for a candidate applying for Medicine and Surgery:

1

JAMB Score: 320

JAMB Points = (320 ÷ 400) × 80= 64

2

O'Level: English A1, Maths B2, Biology A1, Chemistry B3, Physics C4

Points: 6 + 5 + 6 + 4 + 3 = . Average = 24 ÷ 30 = 0.8. O'Level Points = (0.8 ÷ 30) × 20 = 16

3

Total Aggregate = JAMB + O'Level

64 + 16 = 80 / 100 — Excellent! Well above Medicine's estimated cutoff.

Improving your O'Level grades has a disproportionately large impact on your FUHSI aggregate. A single grade improvement — say from C4 to B3 in Chemistry — can add over 1.2 points to your total.

JAMB 280 and Above — Highly Competitive Courses

If you scored 280 or above in JAMB and have strong O'Level grades (mostly A1s and B2s), you are well-positioned for FUHSI's most competitive programmes:

  • Medicine and Surgery — Est. aggregate cutoff 74–79. Nigeria's most sought-after health programme.
  • Nursing Science — Est. aggregate cutoff 67–74. Highly competitive with large applicant pools annually.
  • Physiotherapy — Est. aggregate cutoff 66–70. Growing demand, competitive screening.

JAMB 240–280 — Medium Competitive Courses

With a score in this range and good O'Level grades, you have strong options across several FUHSI programmes:

  • Medical Laboratory Science (MLS) — Est. aggregate 61–67. Excellent career prospects in diagnostics.
  • Pharmacology — Est. aggregate 51–59. Strong demand in pharmaceutical and research sectors.
  • Audiology — Est. aggregate 57–60. Growing specialist field with relatively lower applicant volume.
  • Biochemistry — Est. aggregate 50–51. Versatile health sciences foundation course.

A JAMB score of 270 combined with all A1s and B2s in O'Level can produce an aggregate above 70 — which is enough for most FUHSI programmes except Medicine at peak competition years.

JAMB 180–229 — Moderate Competition Courses

Don't be discouraged by a score in this range. FUHSI offers excellent programmes where you can compete effectively:

  • Microbiology — Est. aggregate 50–50. Important biomedical field with strong research applications.
  • Nutrition and Dietetics — Est. aggregate 55–62. Critical public health discipline.
  • Public Health — Est. aggregate 52–60. Broad career scope in community and population health.
  • Biotechnology & Molecular Biology — Est. aggregate 53–62. Cutting-edge discipline with growing industry demand.

JAMB 140–179 — Accessible Entry Courses

If your JAMB score is in the minimum eligible range, focus on courses with lower competition and leverage exceptional O'Level grades to boost your aggregate:

  • Environmental Health Science — Est. aggregate 52–58. Important public health management discipline.
  • IT & Health Informatics — Est. aggregate 51–53. A technology-focused programme with future-proof career paths.
  • Prosthetics & Orthotics — Est. aggregate 51–59. Specialist clinical technology with limited competition nationally.

Candidates with lower JAMB scores can still achieve competitive aggregates with excellent O'Level grades. If you have mostly A1s and B2s, your O'Level alone contributes up to 55 points to your aggregate score.
